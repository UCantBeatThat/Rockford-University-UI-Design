WEEKLY BUZZ - Aug 08 - 13, 2020

MBA/MCA/MTECH students Celebrate Graduation Day-2020

Horizon College of Engineering & Management celebrated the Graduation Day and conferred degrees to MBA/MCA/MTECH students at Horizon Campus. Shri. Ghanshyam Dass -Senior Advisor, KPMG was the Chief Guest. In his inaugural address, he shared his college day experiences and was nostalgic about the entire ceremony. He advised students to stay focused and committed towards their goal and be always rooted and in touch with their alma mater.

Mr. Manjunath Bhandary, Chairman, Bhandary Foundation, in his introductory remarks said that, No institute is built by Infrastructure or faculty, instead it is built by the students, and also he said that a student’s success and failure is attached to college which plays vital role in upholding the image of the institute. He advised to the graduates to stand for values in which they have been trained at college and get noticed by the management. He concluded stating that the world is very competitive; hence it is very essential to upgrade continuously, which helps one to survive.

Dr. Vishal Samartha, Director, Department of Business administration welcomed the gathering and Dr. U. M. Bhushi, Principal concluded the ceremony by delivering the Vote of Thanks. Ms. Gayathri Shetty, Assistant Professor, Department of Business Administration was the Chief coordinator of the Graduation Day and Ms. Prajwala Preema Rodrigues, Assistant Professor, Department of Business Administration was the host at the event.

Mr. Dayanand Allapur, Vice President & Head HR, Capgemini visits Horizon

Mr. Dayanand Allapur, Vice President & Head HR, Capgemini Business Services India Ltd, Bengaluru and Ms. Swapna Allapur, Vice President - Talent Acquisition and Mobility Leader - Shared Services, Deloitte U.S. India offices visited Horizon and interacted with the Management, faculty members and students.

Mr. Dayanand Allapur, said that one should be updated about the current affairs happening across the globe, latest trends in their respective fields and also stressed that one should adapt to the technological advancements. He said it is important to practically implement the knowledge gained and stated that the application beyond academics is essential. He advised to know the level of competition in the modern world to be at the forefront and also to have a unique perspective of accomplishing the task assigned to them.

Swapna Allapur spoke about the personal branding. She said it is most important for one to identify who they are and their strengths. Further, she insisted to focus on the same and start working towards making it even more efficient. She said one should be always able to adapt to the work culture of the organization they are serving. Youngsters should have the urge and curiosity to learn new things always.

Mr. Deepak Thimaya addresses the attendees of Leadership Development Programme

Department of Business Administration hosted a One-day Leadership Development Programme for UG students. Seventy students from various degree colleges participated in the programme. The day began with a brief inaugural session in the presence of Prof S S Balakrishna, Vice Principal, Dr. Vishal Samartha, Director, Department of Business Administration and Prof Karthik Raj Kudroli, Chief Coordinator of the programme. Prof Karthik welcomed the gathering and highlighted the purpose of the day-long event. Prof S S Balakrishna applauded the efforts of the department for being vibrant in contributing to the society through conducting innovative programmes and also helping in developing leadership skills among students. The first session was taken up by Dr. Vishal Samartha whereby she emphasized on the importance of one’s ability to elevate himself/herself to be a leader. She explained the two nuances of life, Rationality and Emotionality and the path a true leader must take while taking crucial decisions in life. She asserted to her concept through stories and real life experiences. Prof. Sushma V took over the next session and led the students to the real life intricacies and challenges one faces in life. She beckoned the students to chalk out SMART goals and work towards it with commitment. She inspired the students to adopt “Throw the box” strategy and to work towards enhancing one’s personality.

Mr. Deepak Thimaya, eminent television personality, anchor, writer, educationist, film maker, political analyst, advisor, founder of Verbattle addressed the students on the qualities of a good leader. He explained that the inherent qualities of a true leader is his memory, his commitment towards his goal, his Vision and Passion, his ability to recognize the pain and hard work of his followers, to illuminate light in the lives of downtrodden and helpless people. He said a true leader is always well informed, trustworthy, embodiment of truth and must able to communicate and inspire his followers on a personal level. He truly amazed the listeners by his spontaneous flow of thoughts and his experiences.

The fourth session was taken up by Dr. Umesh M Bhushi, Principal. He gave away certain real life cases to students and urged the participants to come up with their analysis and decisions. He motivated students to be committed to their goals and groom themselves to be a better human being. Prof. Mithun Ullal in his session on “Stress the Press” allowed the students to have a practical feel of media and journalism. Students were enthralled by the activity based learning. The day ended with session by Dr. C K Manjunath, Prof. of Humanities & Administrator who enlightened the students on tackling the barriers to leadership.

Our beloved Chairman Manjunath Bhandary addressed the students and asked them to take failures as their reference points instead of blindly following the success stories. He said every person has a leader in him/her. He said every leader must be accountable, committed and responsible for his action and must be able to communicate effectively to his followers. He said a true leader is always disciplined and always transfers his knowledge to others. Finally he thanked all the participants and asked for their actual feedback in order to improvise in the future.

The programme was concluded with a brief valedictory session which included certificate distribution and feedback from participants.

Mr. Chethan Ram speaks on personality development session for faculty members

Mr. Chethan Ram R A, international trainer on personality development, along with Mr. Kumar Anish, an Indian yoga practitioner and personality development trainer from Bangalore, engaged a session on Personality Development for faculty members.

He asked the faculty members to modulate the technique of teaching saying that how matter is delivered, will help in achieving 100 percent results. To be successful he said that a SWOT analysis should be made for each student in the class so that one can deal effectively with them. He stated that if one’s personality is enhanced and if additional knowledge is acquired, students will be motivated to achieve the number one position. In a competitive world it is team work that can achieve this in a different way altogether. The 21st century demands that a leader must `know the way, show the way and walk the way’. Mr. Kumar Anish gave tips to maintain a healthy life style. The audience enjoyed the lively presentation.

Orientation programme held for freshers

An orientation programme, `Success, Motivation & Personality Development’ for freshers of the college was conducted by international trainer on personality development Mr. Chethan Ram R A, along with Mr. Kumar Anish, an Indian yoga practitioner and personality development trainer from Bangalore. Mr. Chethan Ram in his talk said that one should be self-motivated and focused on the goal to become successful. He mentioned that each one of us is a gift of nature. How best we make use of our talent, and give our service to the society is what we are gifting back to the nature.

Prof. S S Balakrishna, Vice Principal, welcomed the gathering and introduced the guests. Dr. U M Bhushi, Principal, urged the students to make best use of the opportunities provided. About 680 students participated in two batches and were greatly inspired by the motivational talks.
